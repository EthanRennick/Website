Dungeons and Dragons is a fantasy tabletop roleplaying game. It allows the player to create their own character with their own story.
They journey and quest in a world created by another player - The Dungeon Master, who controls NPC's and the enemies.

The Dungeons & Dragons roleplaying game is about storytelling in worlds of sword and sorcery. It shares elements with childhood games of make-believe.
Like those games, D&D is driven by imagination. It’s about picturing the towering castle beneath the stormy night sky and imagining how a fantasy
adventurer might react to the challenges that scene presents.

Unlike a game of make-believe, D&D gives structure to the stories, a way of determining the consequences of the adventurers’ action.
Players roll dice to resolve whether their attacks hit or miss or whether their adventurers can scale a cliff, roll away from the
strike of a magical lightning bolt, or pull off some other dangerous task. Anything is possible, but the dice make some outcomes more probable than others.

In the Dungeons & Dragons game, each player creates an adventurer (also called a character) and teams up with other adventurers (played by friends).
Working together, the group might explore a dark dungeon, a ruined city, a haunted castle, a lost temple deep in a jungle, or a lava-filled cavern
beneath a mysterious mountain. The adventurers can solve puzzles, talk with other characters, battle fantastic monsters, and discover fabulous magic
items and other treasure.

Ethan's Favourite D&D Memory

Most of my d&d experience is as a DM, so while I don't have many experiences as a player, I have many as enemies and NPCs.
As I described on my interests page, I love stories and writing. I created many characters that my player's have loved and made art of in their own time.
I've created worlds, npcs and enemies which my players have fought and befriended. Their favourite was an elf called Taycko.
Taycko was cursed and suffered from insanity. He was unpredictable. The favourite moment from the campaign overall was when, during a tense moment, Taycko cast a spell.
A rope fell from the ceiling which led to a pocket dimension. The players climbed the rope and lived in the pocket dimension until they returned to the dungeon.

Great fun for literally anyone.
